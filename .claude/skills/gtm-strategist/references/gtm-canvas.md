## GTM Canvas Fill-in Guide

### Mission & Goals
- **Mission**: What does success look like? (e.g., "Enable 100 freelancers to earn $10K/month")
- **North Star Metric**: Single metric measuring value delivered (e.g., "Weekly active users who send 5+ proposals")
- **GTM Objective**: Specific, measurable goal (e.g., "1,000 paying customers")
- **Timeline**: How long to achieve objective (e.g., "12 months")

### Market
- **TAM**: Total Addressable Market—everyone who could use your solution
- **SAM**: Serviceable Addressable Market—portion you can realistically reach
- **SOM**: Serviceable Obtainable Market—realistic short-term capture
- **Beachhead Segment**: Specific niche to win first
- **Key trends**: Market forces working for/against you

### Early Customer Profile
- **Who**: Demographic/firmographic description
- **Problem**: Primary pain point
- **Current solution**: How they solve it today
- **Job to be done**: Underlying goal
- **Decision maker**: Who has authority to buy
- **Where they learn**: Channels where they seek information
- **Key quote**: Actual customer language about the problem

### Value Proposition
- **UVP**: Core promise (one sentence)
- **USPs**: 2-3 unique selling propositions
- **Key differentiator**: What makes you truly different

### Pricing
- **Model**: Subscription, usage-based, one-time, etc.
- **Price point(s)**: Specific prices for packages
- **Value metric**: What they're paying for
- **Strategy**: Penetration (low price, high volume) vs. Skimming (premium)
- **Target CAC/LTV**: Unit economics targets

### Positioning
- **Category**: What type of product are you?
- **Against**: Key competitors/alternatives
- **We're different because**: Clear differentiation statement
- **Tagline**: Memorable positioning phrase

### Growth Channels
- **Primary channel**: Main acquisition driver (1 channel)
- **Secondary channel**: Supporting channel (1-2 channels)
- **Experimental**: Testing (allocate 10-20% resources)
- **Demand strategy**: Capture (existing demand) vs. Generate (create demand)
- **Growth loop potential**: Possible viral/referral mechanics

### Team & Resources
- **Core team**: Who's dedicated to GTM
- **Key hires**: Skills you need to add
- **External support**: Agencies, contractors, advisors
- **Budget**: Total GTM investment
- **Timeline**: GTM phase duration

### Key Assumptions to Validate
Top 3 most critical assumptions that must be true for GTM success.
These become your first experiments.

## Example: Completed GTM Canvas

**Product**: TaskFlow (Project management for remote freelancers)

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Mission | Help 10,000 freelancers manage projects professionally |
| NSM | Weekly active users with 3+ active projects |
| Objective | 2,000 paying users in 12 months |
| TAM | $2B (project management tools) |
| SAM | $200M (freelancer segment) |
| SOM | $5M (English-speaking solo freelancers) |
| Beachhead | Solo web developers, US-based |
| ECP | Freelance developer, 2-5 clients, $75-150K revenue, uses Notion/Trello messily |
| UVP | "Project management that runs your freelance business, not the other way around" |
| USPs | Auto-invoicing, client portal, time tracking built-in |
| Pricing | $29/month, usage-based (per active project), freemium entry |
| Positioning | "The all-in-one OS for freelance developers"—vs. generic PM tools |
| Primary channel | Content marketing (SEO) |
| Secondary | Dev communities (Reddit, Twitter), Product Hunt launch |
| Growth loop | Client portal creates exposure (collaboration loop) |
| Team | Founder (product), 1 marketer, 1 dev (part-time) |
| Budget | $50K for 12 months |
| Key assumptions | (1) Freelancers will pay $29/mo, (2) SEO can drive 10K monthly visits, (3) Client portal drives referrals |

## OKR Template for GTM

### Quarterly OKR Structure

**Objective**: [Inspiring, qualitative goal]

**Key Results**:
1. [Measurable outcome] → Target: [number]
2. [Measurable outcome] → Target: [number]
3. [Measurable outcome] → Target: [number]

### Example GTM OKRs

**Q1 Objective**: Validate product-market fit with web developer segment

**Key Results**:
1. Complete 30 customer discovery interviews → Learn: Top 3 pain points
2. Achieve 500 free signups from target segment
3. Convert 50 users to paid (10% conversion)
4. NPS score of 40+ from paying users

**Q2 Objective**: Build repeatable acquisition engine

**Key Results**:
1. Publish 20 SEO-optimized blog posts
2. Achieve 5,000 monthly organic visitors
3. Reduce CAC to $75 (from $120)
4. Grow MRR to $5,000
