# Key Questions Library

Comprehensive prompts for startup ideation, organized by method.

## Noticing What's Missing

### Leading Edge Questions
- What field are you at the leading edge of?
- What do you use daily that didn't exist 3 years ago?
- What do power users in your community do that mainstream users don't?
- What tools do you wish existed but don't?
- What's the next logical step in your field that hasn't been built?

### Gap Identification
- What's annoying about the tools you use most?
- What manual process do you repeat weekly?
- What data do you wish you could access but can't?
- What's clunky about how your industry operates?
- Where do you see people improvising solutions?

## Scratching Your Own Itch

### Personal Pain Points
- What problem have you personally experienced this month?
- What task do you procrastinate on because it's painful?
- What would make your daily work 10x easier?
- What do you spend too much time on?
- What frustrates you that others seem to accept?

### Professional Context
- What's broken in your company that you wish you could fix?
- What internal tool do you wish existed?
- What information is hard to find in your organization?
- What process wastes everyone's time?
- What would help you do your job better?

## Schlep Blindness

### Uncovering Hidden Schleps
- What important problem have you dismissed as "too hard"?
- What tedious work do people avoid in your industry?
- What's everyone's least favorite part of their job?
- What problems require getting your hands dirty?
- What would you build if difficulty didn't matter?

### Industry Schleps
- What industries are stuck in the past?
- What businesses still run on spreadsheets and phone calls?
- What processes involve too much manual coordination?
- Where are experts doing work that could be systematized?
- What regulated industries need modernization?

## Riding the Wave

### Technology Trends
- What's newly possible that wasn't 2 years ago?
- What expensive thing is becoming cheap?
- What complex thing is becoming simple?
- What's AI/ML making feasible for the first time?
- What infrastructure improvements create new possibilities?

### Behavioral Shifts
- How are people's habits changing?
- What do younger users do that older users don't?
- What's becoming socially acceptable that wasn't before?
- What expectations are rising?
- What friction are people no longer willing to tolerate?

### Market Shifts
- What regulations are creating new opportunities?
- What large companies are losing touch with users?
- What demographics are underserved?
- What markets are fragmenting or consolidating?
- Where is there a gap between supply and demand?

## Making the Unsexy Sexy

### Boring Industries
- What industries are considered unsexy but essential?
- What do people need but don't brag about?
- What problems are embarrassing to talk about?
- What industries have low prestige but high profit?
- What do people pay for silently?

### Undervalued Problems
- What problems do VCs overlook?
- What ideas sound too simple to be valuable?
- What needs seem too niche to pursue?
- What solutions seem too obvious?
- What's dismissed as a "feature, not a product"?

## Targeting Specific Users

### User Identification
- Who has this problem most acutely?
- Who would use this even if it's rough?
- Who's currently hacking together a solution?
- Who complains about this publicly?
- Who's paying for inferior alternatives?

### Urgency Testing
- Who needs this solved in the next 30 days?
- Who's actively looking for solutions?
- Who's in pain right now?
- Who would switch from their current solution immediately?
- Who would be a reference customer?

## Competition Analysis

### Market Assessment
- What do people use today?
- Why hasn't this been solved well?
- What's wrong with existing solutions?
- Where are incumbents complacent?
- What do users complain about with current options?

### Differentiation
- What would a 10x better solution look like?
- What trade-offs would you make differently?
- What user segment is underserved?
- What technology advantage could you have?
- What insight do you have that others lack?

## Founder Fit

### Capability Check
- Can you build a prototype this month?
- Do you understand the users deeply?
- Do you have relevant domain knowledge?
- Can you iterate faster than competitors?
- What unfair advantage do you have?

### Motivation Check
- Would you work on this for 5+ years?
- Are you excited about this problem?
- Do you genuinely want this to exist?
- Would you work on this without funding?
- Is this aligned with your long-term goals?
